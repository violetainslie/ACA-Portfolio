NCSU Profile:

North Carolina State University was founded in 1887 and, since then, has been ranked in the top 1% of universities worldwide. In order to transfer to NCSU, you need to declare an intended major. For Computer Engineering, you need to have taken ENG 111 and 112, Calculus I and II (and have a 2.5 GPA in the last two calculus or higher math courses), Calculus-based Physics, and General Chemistry with lab, in addition to a 3.0 or above cumulative GPA. Applications for transfer students open August 1st and close February 15th. The student body of NCSU is more than 39,000, with an average class size of 36. The campus is located in downtown Raleigh on 2,099 acres of land. Tuiton for living off campus is $28,668 for in-state and $54,191 for out-of-state (North Carolina State University, 2025).

The Goodnight Scholarships office has a scholarship for transfer students valued at $24,000 per year for six semesters. NCSU lists specific recommendations for being a competitive applications; by not following these recommendations you could be putting yourself at a disadvantage, as others may.
